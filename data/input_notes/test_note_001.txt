The patient is a 34-year-old man with drug-resistant epilepsy since childhood. Six-month postoperative follow-up after VNS implantation. Before VNS implantation, he was having approximately 12 seizures per month. At this 6-month follow-up, he and his wife report an approximately 50% reduction in seizure frequency. VNS settings were reviewed today. He reports no missed doses of levetiracetam and lacosamide. Seizure frequency is not increasing. His seizures are predominantly generalized tonic-clonic seizures, and several have occurred out of sleep. He lives with his wife and does not live alone. He reports mild short-term memory difficulty but denies problems with attention or concentration. He reports mild anxiety related to breakthrough seizures. He denies depression, psychosis, hallucinations, alcohol use disorder, and substance use disorder. The surgical incision is clean, dry, and intact without infection, drainage, dehiscence, or other wound complication. He has mild residual postoperative neck pain controlled with acetaminophen. The device is functioning normally, with no evidence of lead migration, lead fracture, or battery depletion. He denies tingling, painful stimulation, or sleep disruption from stimulation. He reports transient hoarseness during VNS stimulation. He denies dizziness, rash, and cognitive slowing. Since the lacosamide dose increase, he reports daytime fatigue.
