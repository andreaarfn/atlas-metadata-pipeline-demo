The patient is a 42-year-old woman with focal epilepsy for more than 20 years. RNS programming follow-up visit. Prior to RNS implantation, she was experiencing daily seizures despite multiple antiseizure medications. Since RNS implantation two years ago, seizure frequency has substantially improved, although she continues to have occasional breakthrough events. She reports excellent medication adherence and denies missed doses. Seizure frequency has remained stable over the last six months. She lives alone. She reports that many of her seizures occur during sleep. She denies memory problems. She reports worsening attention and concentration over the past year. Processing speed is otherwise unchanged. She also describes difficulty organizing complex tasks at work. Psychiatric history is notable for ADHD. She denies depression, anxiety, psychosis, alcohol use disorder, and substance use disorder. There are no surgical wound concerns, postoperative pain complaints, or evidence of infection. The neurostimulator is functioning appropriately without evidence of lead fracture, lead migration, battery depletion, or other hardware malfunction. She reports intermittent tingling sensations immediately after stimulation but denies painful stimulation and denies sleep disruption from stimulation. Following the most recent cenobamate dose increase, she reports dizziness and fatigue. She denies rash, medication toxicity, and cognitive slowing.
